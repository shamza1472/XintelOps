## [THE PIPELINE — 2:00–6:00]

### STAGE 1: SOURCE COLLECTION

*[VISUALS: Animated diagram showing sources feeding into a central engine. Reuters, AP, BBC, Al Jazeera, NATO, DoD, UN, OFAC, Crisis Group, CSIS, Carnegie, RAND — all lighting up.]*

**HOST:**

> Every scan cycle starts with a sweep across more than 20 curated, high-trust sources.

> We're pulling from **direct wire feeds** — Reuters, AP, BBC, Al Jazeera, TASS, IRNA.
>
> We're pulling from **official government and institutional channels** — the Pentagon, NATO press releases, the UN, the EU Council, the US Treasury's OFAC sanctions database.
>
> We're pulling from **the world's top think tanks** — CSIS, Carnegie Endowment, RAND, Atlantic Council, IISS, Brookings, Chatham House, SIPRI, MEI.
>
> We run six targeted **Google News queries** across active theaters and domains — military conflict, diplomacy, sanctions, Indo-Pacific, the Middle East, Eastern Europe.
>
> And we're even monitoring **credible open-source communities** on Reddit — CredibleDefense, Geopolitics, World News — where ground-level signals sometimes emerge *before* wire services pick them up.

> **In a single scan cycle, the engine processes thousands of data points from across the entire global information landscape.**

---

### STAGE 2: VERIFICATION

*[VISUALS: Red/green signal indicators. Confidence bars. "CRISIS FLAG: TRUE" appearing on screen.]*

**HOST:**

> Raw signals mean nothing without verification.
>
> This is where XIntelOps separates itself from a news aggregator.
>
> Every signal that enters the pipeline is evaluated on three criteria:
>
> **Confidence** — Is this corroborated by multiple Tier 1 sources, or is it a single-source claim?
>
> **Misinfo Risk** — Does this carry the hallmarks of state-sponsored narrative manipulation or uncorroborated assertion?
>
> **Actionability** — Is this operationally significant, or is it background noise?

> Signals that fail these checks are **blocked** — and logged as blocked. Every scan cycle reports exactly how many signals were scraped, verified, and blocked.
>
> What survives? *Only what's real. Only what matters.*

> And when the system detects a crisis indicator — a strike, a mobilization, a diplomatic rupture, a ceasefire collapse — it doesn't just flag it.
>
> It activates **Crisis Mode.**

>
> It's built directly into the XIntelOps pipeline — so that what reaches the analyst is not just fast. It's **defensible.**
